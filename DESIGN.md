## Overview
Pearlora is a polished travel booking experience for premium Sri Lanka stays. The system balances luminous photography, warm gold accents, and calm navy surfaces with a restrained, high-end layout. It feels curated, trustworthy, and quietly luxurious.

## Color
The palette centers on a deep navy primary tone with champagne-gold accents and soft pearl neutrals. Primary navy anchors brand credibility, while gold highlights CTA and trust signals. Pearl ivory surfaces keep the interface luminous and easy to scan.

## Typography
The visual system combines an elegant display voice in `Playfair Display` for headlines with the clean readability of `Plus Jakarta Sans` for body text and controls. Headlines are generous and expressive; body copy is relaxed and highly legible.

## Layout
Use wide content margins, generous section spacing, and layered cards to create an uncluttered premium hierarchy. The layout relies on clear groupings, soft drop shadows, and bordered surfaces to define sections without heavy visual weight.

## Components
- Button: rounded capsule or soft pill shape, with primary dark/navy and gold accent styles.
- Card: white surface with subtle border and elevated shadow, rounding at `16px` or `24px`.
- Input: soft-edge fields with gentle focus glow, clear labels, and calm surface fill.
- Status pill: compact rounded badge with color-coded states for booking status.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do use navy and champagne gold as the core brand contrast.
- Do keep forms and booking summaries clean, direct, and easy to scan.
- Do reinforce trust with simple status labels and clear booking details.
- Don't use noisy bargain-travel or flash-sale marketing patterns.
- Don't use dark neon interfaces, gimmicky gradients, or cluttered OTA-style comparison tables.
- Don't turn the interface into a technical admin dashboard; keep the experience elegant and travel-focused.
